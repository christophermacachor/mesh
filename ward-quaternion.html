On Quaternions; or on a New System of Imaginaries in Algebra
Sir William Rowan Hamilton
Philosophical Magazine, Series 3, Vol. 25, No. 163, pp. 10–13 (1843)
The original quaternion formulation: a four-dimensional number system q = a + bi + cj + dk where i² = j² = k² = ijk = −1. Hamilton's discovery on Brougham Bridge, Dublin, carved the fundamental relation into stone. This non-commutative algebra naturally encodes 3D rotations without the singularities of Euler angles. The scalar part a commutes with all elements — it is the abelian center of the quaternion algebra.
